**Hirtz & Carter – Discovery Questionnaire**  
*Project: Automated Image‑Quality‑Assurance (Image QA) for retail product photos*  

Below are the questions you should ask during the discovery session.  
Each question is followed by a short note on **why it matters** and the **red‑flags** that, if raised, indicate a potential risk or a need for a deeper technical solution (e.g., custom training, extra infrastructure, tighter compliance, etc.).

---

## 1. Business Requirements  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| B1 | **What specific business outcomes are you hoping to achieve with an automated Image‑QA system?** (e.g., faster time‑to‑market, lower re‑work cost, compliance avoidance, higher conversion) | Aligns the technical solution to ROI and helps prioritize features (speed vs. explainability vs. auditability). | “Just want a “nice‑to‑have” tool” → low budget/commitment; “Must avoid any marketplace penalties” → high compliance need. |
| B2 | **How many product images are created each month, and what is the expected growth rate over the next 12‑24 months?** | Determines throughput, storage, and scaling requirements. | > 200 k/month now **or** > 30 % YoY growth → need robust auto‑scaling and GPU reservation planning. |
| B3 | **Which teams will be the primary users of the QA results (e.g., merchandisers, retouchers, compliance, marketplace ops)?** | Influences UI/UX, level of explanation needed, and who must sign‑off on results. | Users are “non‑technical” → need clear, plain‑language explanations; “audit team” → need detailed logs & traceability. |
| B4 | **What is the current cost (person‑hours / dollars) of manual image QA, and what cost‑saving target do you have for automation?** | Provides a baseline for measuring ROI and justifies the investment. | No clear baseline → harder to quantify ROI; target saving < 10 % may not justify a full AI solution. |
| B5 | **Are there any regulatory or marketplace‑specific image rules (e.g., Amazon, Walmart) that must be enforced automatically?** | Determines the need for rule‑based checks vs. pure model‑based reasoning and the level of auditability required. | “Must meet Amazon’s 100 % compliance” → high penalty for false‑negatives → stricter accuracy & explainability needed. |
| B6 | **How often do your image‑quality standards change (new brand guidelines, seasonal styles, new marketplaces)?** | Frequent changes favor a frontier VLM that can be re‑prompted rather than re‑training a custom model. | Standards change **monthly** → VLM is a better fit; standards are static → custom model could be considered. |
| B7 | **Do you need the system to provide a “pass/fail” decision only, or also a ranked list of defect types with severity?** | Affects output schema and whether a single VLM pass is enough or you need multiple specialized detectors. | Need granular severity scores → may require additional detection models or post‑processing. |

## 2. Technical Requirements  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| T1 | **What is the maximum acceptable latency for a single image to be evaluated (including any post‑processing)?** | Determines whether a frontier VLM (≈ 1‑2 s per image) is sufficient or if a faster, lighter model is required. | Latency < 200 ms → VLM alone may be too slow; latency ≤ 5 s is fine for VLM. |
| T2 | **What throughput (images per second/minute) do you anticipate during peak publishing cycles?** | Drives GPU count, auto‑scaling configuration, and cost estimation. | > 10 imgs/sec → need ≥ 3 standard GPUs or performance tier; < 2 imgs/sec → a single GPU may suffice. |
| T3 | **Do you need explanations for each QA decision (e.g., “background not pure white – 85 % confidence”) ?** | VLMs can generate natural‑language rationales; detection models provide visual evidence. | No explanation needed → could drop VLM and use pure classifiers for cost savings. |
| T4 | **What are your security, data‑privacy, and compliance requirements for the image data (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, PCI‑DSS)?** | Influences whether data can be sent to cloud inference, need for encryption, or on‑prem deployment. | Must keep data on‑prem or in a private VPC → may need dedicated GPU reservation or hybrid deployment. |
| T5 | **Do you have any existing ML/AI infrastructure (e.g., on‑prem GPUs, MLOps pipelines, data lakes) you want to reuse?** | Helps decide whether to use Clarifai’s managed compute or integrate with current stack. | Existing on‑prem GPU farm → could leverage but would add integration effort; no infra → fully managed is easier. |
| T6 | **What is your preferred deployment model: fully managed SaaS, private cloud, or on‑premise?** | Determines compute reservation options and potential licensing. | Preference for on‑prem → need custom deployment, possibly higher cost. |
| T7 | **Do you anticipate needing to run batch jobs (e.g., bulk retro‑fit of legacy catalog) in addition to real‑time checks?** | Batch processing may be scheduled on cheaper spot‑instances; real‑time needs reserved capacity. | Large historic catalog > 10 M images → need batch pipeline and cost‑control strategy. |
| T8 | **Are there any constraints on the image size or format that could affect model input (e.g., max 5 MB, only JPEG)?** | Guides pre‑processing (resize, convert) and model selection (some VLMs have max resolution). | Images > 4 K resolution → may need down‑sampling before VLM, affecting accuracy. |
| T9 | **Do you require any multi‑modal inputs (e.g., a textual brief alongside the image) for the QA decision?** | VLMs can ingest both image + text; a pure CV model cannot. | Yes → VLM is a natural fit. |
| T10 | **How tolerant are you of occasional false‑positives vs. false‑negatives?** (e.g., “Is it worse to reject a good image or to let a bad image through?”) | Influences threshold setting and whether to prioritize recall or precision. | High penalty for false‑negatives (regulatory) → set high recall, possibly add human‑in‑the‑loop. |

## 3. Data Requirements  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| D1 | **What volume of labeled (good / bad) images do you currently have, and how are the labels stored?** | Needed for model evaluation, fine‑tuning (if ever required), and establishing a validation set. | < 500 labeled samples → may limit ability to benchmark VLM reliably; may need to create labeling effort. |
| D2 | **What labeling schema do you use (e.g., defect categories, severity levels, SKU mapping)?** | Determines the JSON output structure and whether the VLM can directly emit those fields. | Very granular taxonomy (20+ defect types) → may need custom post‑processing or multiple detectors. |
| D3 | **Are the images stored in a DAM, on S3, on‑prem file server, or elsewhere?** | Impacts integration method (webhook vs. polling) and data transfer latency. | Distributed across many locations → integration complexity rises. |
| D4 | **Do you have any image‑level metadata (e.g., SKU, source platform, photographer) that should be passed through the QA pipeline?** | Allows traceability and linking QA results back to business objects. | No metadata → you’ll need to generate or enrich it before processing. |
| D5 | **What is the quality of the existing data (e.g., compression artifacts, inconsistent lighting) and does it reflect the worst‑case scenarios you expect?** | Helps assess whether the VLM will generalize or if you need domain‑specific fine‑tuning. | Data is already heavily compressed → may degrade model accuracy; consider higher‑resolution source. |
| D6 | **Are there any privacy or copyright constraints on the images (e.g., brand‑protected, licensed assets)?** | Determines whether images can be sent to a public endpoint or need on‑prem inference. | Strict copyright → on‑prem or private VPC required. |
| D7 | **How will new images be added to the system (batch upload, real‑time API, CMS sync)?** | Guides the design of the ingestion pipeline. | Real‑time API from multiple sources → need robust queueing and idempotency handling. |
| D8 | **Do you have a “golden” reference set of images that represent the ideal quality for each product type?** | Useful for similarity checks and for the VLM to compare against (prompt with reference). | No reference set → may need to build one as part of the project. |

## 4. Integration Requirements  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| I1 | **Which e‑commerce platform(s) or DAM(s) does the catalog live in (e.g., Shopify, Magento, Adobe Experience Manager, custom CMS)?** | Determines API compatibility, authentication method, and possible SDK usage. | Custom CMS with no API → may need a wrapper service. |
| I2 | **What is the preferred method for receiving QA results (REST webhook, message queue, direct DB write, UI dashboard)?** | Aligns with existing workflow and ensures teams can act on the results promptly. | Only manual CSV export possible → may limit real‑time automation. |
| I3 | **Do you need the system to automatically trigger downstream actions (e.g., move image to “re‑work” folder, notify retoucher, reject upload)?** | Defines orchestration logic and required integrations (e.g., Slack, email, task‑manager). | No automation allowed → solution becomes a reporting tool only. |
| I4 | **What authentication/authorization standards are in place for internal APIs (OAuth2, API keys, SAML, etc.)?** | Ensures secure integration and compliance with corporate policies. | Only legacy API keys with no rotation → may need to implement a token‑gateway. |
| I5 | **Is there an existing monitoring/alerting stack (Datadog, New Relic, Prometheus) you would like the AI service to feed into?** | Allows seamless ops hand‑off and SLA tracking. | No monitoring → you’ll need to set up basic health‑check endpoints. |
| I6 | **Do you require a user interface for reviewers to view, edit, or override AI decisions?** | Influences UI development effort and whether a simple webhook is enough. | Yes → need a lightweight dashboard or integration with existing QA UI. |
| I7 | **Are there any batch‑processing tools (e.g., Airflow, Prefect) already in use for catalog updates?** | Could be leveraged to schedule large‑scale retro‑fits. | No orchestrator → may need to build a simple scheduler. |

## 5. Timeline & Budget  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| TL1 | **What is your target go‑live date for the MVP (minimum viable product) and for full production?** | Drives the pacing of model evaluation, integration, and testing phases. | Target < 4 weeks → may need to limit scope to a single VLM and skip custom UI. |
| TL2 | **Are there any hard deadlines (e.g., upcoming seasonal catalog launch, marketplace compliance deadline)?** | May require fast‑track delivery or additional resources. | Imminent deadline → prioritize core “pass/fail” functionality first. |
| TL3 | **What budget range have you allocated for this project (including compute reservation, licensing, consulting)?** | Helps size the GPU reservation (standard vs. performance tier) and decide whether a custom‑trained model is affordable. | Budget < $15 k for year‑1 → likely stay with managed VLM only; higher budget allows reserved GPUs & custom fine‑tuning. |
| TL4 | **Do you prefer a fixed‑price engagement, time‑and‑materials, or a usage‑based model?** | Impacts contract structure and risk sharing. | Preference for fixed price → need clear scope and success metrics. |
| TL5 | **Will you fund a proof‑of‑concept (PoC) separately before the full rollout?** | Allows risk mitigation and early validation of model suitability. | No PoC budget → may need to embed PoC cost within the main contract. |
| TL6 | **What internal approval process is required before resources (GPU reservation, data access) can be provisioned?** | Determines lead time for procurement and compliance sign‑off. | Multi‑level approvals → add buffer to timeline. |

## 6. Success Criteria  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| S1 | **What quantitative metrics will define success (e.g., ≥ 90 % accuracy, ≤ 2 % false‑negative rate, < 5 s latency, X % reduction in manual QA time)?** | Provides clear targets for model evaluation and SLA definition. | Metrics are vague (“improve quality”) → need to refine to measurable KPIs. |
| S2 | **Who will sign off on the final solution (e.g., VP of Merchandising, Compliance Officer, IT Director)?** | Identifies decision‑makers for go‑live and for acceptance testing. | No clear owner → risk of delayed sign‑off. |
| S3 | **How will you validate that the AI’s decisions are trustworthy (e.g., periodic human audit, confidence‑threshold reviews)?** | Determines the need for human‑in‑the‑loop and audit logs. | No audit plan → may fail compliance or stakeholder confidence. |
| S4 | **What is the acceptable tolerance for false‑positives vs. false‑negatives in the context of your business risk?** | Helps set decision thresholds and decide whether to favor recall or precision. | Tolerance not defined → could lead to mis‑aligned thresholds later. |
| S5 | **Do you expect the solution to generate periodic performance reports (e.g., weekly defect trends, ROI calculations)?** | Drives dashboard/reporting requirements. | No reporting requirement → may miss opportunities for continuous improvement. |
| S6 | **What is the plan for ongoing model monitoring and re‑training (if needed) after deployment?** | Ensures long‑term reliability as product styles evolve. | No monitoring plan → risk of model drift. |

---

### How to Use This Questionnaire  

1. **Start with Business & Success sections** to secure executive buy‑in and define ROI.  
2. **Move to Technical & Data sections** to size the compute reservation and decide whether a frontier VLM alone will meet the needs.  
3. **Follow with Integration, Timeline & Budget** to align engineering effort with the customer’s delivery cadence.  
4. **Capture red‑flags** in a risk register; they will guide whether you need:  
   * a simple VLM‑only pipeline (most common for dynamic QA),  
   * additional detection models for visual localisation, or  
   * a custom‑trained model if the VLM cannot meet the required accuracy.  

Feel free to adapt, add follow‑up probes, or reorder the questions based on the flow of your conversation. Good luck!

## Clarifai Compute Orchestration Discovery

### Model Selection Discovery

**1. Can a Vision-Language Model (VLM) solve this?**
- Can you describe what a human would look for when evaluating these images?
- Is this something a knowledgeable person could assess without specialized training?
- Do you need the AI to explain WHY it made a decision?

**2. What are your latency and throughput requirements?**
- What response time is acceptable? (1-5 sec = VLMs work great, <100ms = may need optimization)
- How many images/documents per second at peak?
- Is batch processing acceptable or must it be real-time?

**3. How often do your requirements change?**
- Will the rules/criteria for evaluation evolve over time?
- How quickly do you need to adapt to new requirements?
- (Frequent changes favor VLMs - update via prompts, no retraining)

**4. What accuracy is required?**
- What false positive/negative rates are acceptable?
- Is 90-95% accuracy sufficient, or do you need 99%+?
- How will edge cases be handled?
