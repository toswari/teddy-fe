**Discovery Questionnaire – Hirtz & Carter (Image‑QA System for Retail)**  

Below is a ready‑to‑use list of questions, grouped by the six discovery categories you asked for.  
For each question you’ll see:

* **What to ask** – the exact wording you can use with the client.  
* **Why it matters** – the business or technical reason the answer is critical for solution design.  
* **Red‑flags** – warning signs in the response that may indicate hidden risk, missing capability, or a need for deeper follow‑up.

---

## 1. Business Requirements – Understanding the problem, impact & stakeholders  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | *“Can you walk me through a typical end‑to‑end workflow for a product image from capture/ingest to publishing on your site or marketplace?”* | Reveals where the QA step will sit, who owns each step, and where automation can deliver the biggest ROI. | Workflow is manual‑only, no defined gate for QA, or the “publish” step is outside their control (e.g., third‑party agency). |
| 2 | *“What business outcomes are you hoping to achieve with automated image QA (e.g., faster time‑to‑market, reduced re‑work cost, higher conversion rates, compliance with marketplace standards)?”* | Connects the technical solution to measurable KPIs and helps justify budget. | Vague outcomes (“just want it to work”) or no quantifiable targets. |
| 3 | *“Who will be the primary users of the QA system (e.g., photographers, retouchers, merchandisers, marketplace ops) and what decisions will they take based on the model’s output?”* | Determines UI/UX, permission levels, and the need for explanations vs. binary pass/fail. | No clear owner, or users expect the system to *make* final publishing decisions without human sign‑off. |
| 4 | *“How many product images do you ingest each week/month, and what is the expected growth rate of the catalog over the next 12‑24 months?”* | Drives throughput, storage, and compute sizing; also informs future‑proofing. | Answers like “we don’t know” or “we’ll double every month” without capacity planning. |
| 5 | *“Do you have any regulatory or marketplace‑specific compliance rules that images must satisfy (e.g., Amazon image standards, brand‑guideline audits)?”* | Determines rule‑based checks that may need to be baked in or verified in addition to AI detection. | “No compliance rules” – may indicate missing documentation that later becomes a blocker. |
| 6 | *“Would you ever need the system to suggest corrective actions (e.g., ‘increase brightness by 10 %’, ‘remove background shadow’) or only flag problems?”* | Influences whether you need a generative/repair component or a pure detection model. | Expectation of full‑auto‑repair without willingness to invest in additional tooling. |
| 7 | *“How do you currently measure the cost of image defects (e.g., re‑retouch hours, lost sales, marketplace penalties)?”* | Provides a baseline to calculate ROI and to set success thresholds. | No tracking of defect cost → hard to prove value later. |

## 2. Technical Requirements – Infrastructure, performance, security, and model suitability  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | *“What is the maximum latency you can tolerate for a single image QA check (e.g., < 500 ms, < 2 s, batch‑only)?”* | Determines whether a frontier multimodal model (typically 1‑3 s per 1024×1024 image on GPU) is acceptable or if a lighter custom model is needed. | Requirement < 100 ms – frontier model may be too slow without model‑specific acceleration. |
| 2 | *“Do you need real‑time (on‑the‑fly) QA as images are uploaded, or is batch processing overnight acceptable?”* | Guides deployment topology (edge vs. cloud) and compute provisioning. | Real‑time + high throughput + strict latency → may need on‑prem or dedicated inference hardware. |
| 3 | *“What is your preferred deployment environment – public cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure), private/on‑prem, or hybrid?”* | Affects model licensing, data residency, and compute‑cost calculations. | Preference for on‑prem but no GPU hardware – would require capital investment. |
| 4 | *“Are there any security, data‑privacy, or compliance constraints that would prevent sending images to a third‑party AI service (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, internal policies)?”* | Determines whether you can use hosted frontier models (e.g., Clarifai’s API) or need a self‑hosted version. | “All images must stay on‑prem” → must run model locally. |
| 5 | *“Do you need explainability for each flagged issue (e.g., heat‑map, textual reason) to satisfy reviewers or auditors?”* | Frontier multimodal models can return visual explanations; custom models may need extra engineering. | No need for explanations → you can simplify the pipeline, but may miss stakeholder acceptance. |
| 6 | *“What is the expected throughput (images per second/minute) during peak upload periods?”* | Directly feeds GPU count and scaling strategy. | Throughput > 500 img/sec – frontier model may need model parallelism or custom lightweight model. |
| 7 | *“Do you already have an ML/AI platform (e.g., SageMaker, Vertex AI, Azure ML) that you would like us to integrate with?”* | Reduces integration effort and leverages existing CI/CD pipelines. | No platform – you’ll need to provision and manage the whole stack. |
| 8 | *“Will the system need to support multiple image resolutions or formats (RAW, JPEG, PNG, TIFF, PSD) and automatically resize/convert before analysis?”* | Affects preprocessing pipeline and model input constraints. | Expectation to ingest RAW at full resolution without preprocessing – may exceed model limits. |

## 3. Data Requirements – Availability, labeling, quality, and governance  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | *“What volume of historical images do you have that are already labeled as ‘pass’ or ‘fail’ according to your QA checklists?”* | Labeled data can be used to fine‑tune a frontier model or to evaluate baseline performance. | < 100 labeled samples → limited ability to validate or improve accuracy. |
| 2 | *“How are those labels captured (manual QC logs, spreadsheets, metadata tags) and in what format?”* | Determines effort needed to ingest and map labels to the model’s schema. | Labels stored in inconsistent free‑text notes → high cleaning effort. |
| 3 | *“Do you have a set of reference images or style‑guide assets (e.g., brand colour swatches, background templates) that the model should compare against?”* | Enables rule‑based checks or prompt engineering for frontier models. | No reference assets – harder to define objective criteria. |
| 4 | *“Are any of your images subject to privacy or copyright restrictions (e.g., third‑party brand logos, user‑generated content) that would limit model training or cloud storage?”* | Influences data handling, anonymization, and licensing. | “All images are public domain” – good; otherwise need extra compliance work. |
| 5 | *“What is the typical image resolution, colour profile (RGB/CMYK), and file size you receive from photographers/retouchers?”* | Impacts preprocessing and model input size decisions. | Very large RAW files (> 30 MP) – may need down‑sampling before inference. |
| 6 | *“How do you plan to capture new images and their QA outcomes moving forward (e.g., automated logging, manual entry, API)?*” | Ensures a continuous feedback loop for model monitoring and improvement. | No plan → model drift will go unnoticed. |
| 7 | *“Do you have any existing data‑quality issues (e.g., mislabeled images, duplicate files, corrupted assets) that we should be aware of?”* | Helps set expectations for initial accuracy and cleaning effort. | High rate of corrupted files → may require robust validation pipeline. |
| 8 | *“Would you be open to augmenting your dataset with synthetic variations (e.g., lighting changes, background swaps) to improve model robustness?”* | Provides a path to boost performance without costly manual labeling. | Reluctance to synthetic data – may limit accuracy gains. |

## 4. Integration Requirements – Connecting the QA engine to existing tools & workflows  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | *“Which e‑commerce platform(s) do you use to publish product images (e.g., Shopify, Magento, Salesforce Commerce Cloud, custom CMS)?”* | Determines API compatibility and where the QA decision will be injected. | Custom legacy CMS with no API – integration will require custom adapters. |
| 2 | *“Do you already have a Digital Asset Management (DAM) system? If so, what APIs or webhooks does it expose?”* | Allows the QA service to pull images directly and push back status flags. | No DAM – images are stored on shared drives → higher integration effort. |
| 3 | *“What is the preferred method for receiving QA results – REST API response, webhook notification, batch CSV, or UI dashboard?”* | Shapes the design of the output interface and any downstream automation. | Expectation of a fully custom UI without using existing dashboards – may increase scope. |
| 4 | *“Will the QA system need to interact with your existing retouching/approval tools (e.g., Adobe Photoshop scripts, InDesign, internal ticketing system)?”* | Enables automatic ticket creation or auto‑routing for failed images. | No integration plan – manual copy‑paste may hinder adoption. |
| 5 | *“Do you need role‑based access control (RBAC) for who can view, edit, or override QA decisions?”* | Important for governance and audit trails. | No RBAC – potential compliance risk. |
| 6 | *“Are there any existing CI/CD pipelines for deploying ML models that you would like us to plug into?”* | Facilitates continuous improvement and versioning. | No pipelines – you’ll have to set up model deployment from scratch. |
| 7 | *“Do you require the system to generate any compliance reports (e.g., daily QA summary, marketplace audit logs) automatically?”* | Determines reporting capabilities and data aggregation needs. | No reporting requirement – may miss opportunity for visibility. |

## 5. Timeline & Budget – Project constraints and financial expectations  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | *“What is the target go‑live date for the automated image QA solution?”* | Sets the pacing for proof‑of‑concept, development, testing, and rollout. | Target is < 4 weeks – may be unrealistic for full integration and data prep. |
| 2 | *“Are there any hard deadlines (e.g., upcoming seasonal catalog launch, marketplace audit) that could accelerate or delay the project?”* | Helps prioritize features (e.g., batch‑only vs. real‑time). | Immediate deadline with incomplete data – risk of scope cut. |
| 3 | *“Do you have a budget range allocated for this initiative (including licensing, compute, and services)?”* | Guides whether a managed frontier‑model subscription is feasible vs. a custom‑built solution. | No budget disclosed – may need to propose phased approach. |
| 4 | *“Will this effort be treated as a proof‑of‑concept/pilot, a minimum viable product (MVP), or a full production deployment?”* | Determines the level of robustness, SLA, and monitoring required. | Ambiguous – risk of under‑ or over‑engineering. |
| 5 | *“Do you anticipate additional spend for GPU hardware or cloud compute beyond the AI service subscription?”* | Clarifies total cost of ownership and any capital‑expenditure constraints. | ‘We don’t have any GPU budget’ – may limit on‑prem options. |
| 6 | *“How will the project be staffed on your side (e.g., product owner, data engineer, QA lead) and what level of involvement is expected?”* | Ensures you have the right points of contact for requirements gathering and testing. | No dedicated owner – could cause delays in decision‑making. |

## 6. Success Criteria – Measuring impact and defining “done”  

| # | Question | Why it matters | Red‑flags |
|---|----------|----------------|-----------|
| 1 | *“What quantitative metrics will you use to judge the AI‑QA system’s success (e.g., % reduction in manual re‑work, average QA latency, accuracy > X % on a held‑out test set)?”* | Provides clear targets for model performance and operational KPIs. | Vague metrics like “it should be good” – need concrete numbers. |
| 2 | *“What is the minimum acceptable precision/recall (or false‑positive/false‑negative) rate for each defect type (e.g., background shadows, color shift)?”* | Determines whether frontier model performance meets business tolerances. | No tolerance thresholds – risk of deploying a model that generates too many false alerts. |
| 3 | *“How frequently would you like to review model performance (e.g., weekly, monthly) and who will be responsible for that review?”* | Sets up monitoring and continuous improvement processes. | No monitoring plan – model drift may go unnoticed. |
| 4 | *“What would be a deal‑breaker that would make you consider the project a failure (e.g., missed compliance penalties, cost overruns, user adoption < 50 %)?”* | Highlights critical risk areas to address early. | No identified deal‑breakers – may indicate lack of risk awareness. |
| 5 | *“Do you expect the system to generate any business value beyond defect detection (e.g., insights on common image issues, recommendations for photographer training)?”* | Opens the door for value‑added analytics that can increase ROI. | No interest in insights – may limit future expansion. |
| 6 | *“Will you require a formal hand‑off document or knowledge‑transfer session once the solution is live?”* | Ensures sustainability and internal ownership. | No hand‑off plan – risk of dependency on the vendor. |

### How to Use This List
1. **Start with Business & Success questions** – they build the case and get stakeholder buy‑in.  
2. **Move to Technical & Data** – once you know the impact, you can size the compute and decide if a frontier multimodal model (e.g., CLIP‑based vision‑language) is sufficient or if a custom‑trained model is needed.  
3. **Address Integration, Timeline & Budget** – to lock down delivery logistics.  
4. **Document red‑flags** as you hear answers; they become the basis for risk mitigation or a proposal of a phased approach.

Feel free to adjust wording to match the tone of your discovery call. Good luck!

## Clarifai Compute Orchestration Discovery

### Model Selection Discovery

**1. Can a Vision-Language Model (VLM) solve this?**
- Can you describe what a human would look for when evaluating these images?
- Is this something a knowledgeable person could assess without specialized training?
- Do you need the AI to explain WHY it made a decision?

**2. What are your latency and throughput requirements?**
- What response time is acceptable? (1-5 sec = VLMs work great, <100ms = may need optimization)
- How many images/documents per second at peak?
- Is batch processing acceptable or must it be real-time?

**3. How often do your requirements change?**
- Will the rules/criteria for evaluation evolve over time?
- How quickly do you need to adapt to new requirements?
- (Frequent changes favor VLMs - update via prompts, no retraining)

**4. What accuracy is required?**
- What false positive/negative rates are acceptable?
- Is 90-95% accuracy sufficient, or do you need 99%+?
- How will edge cases be handled?

### GPU & Infrastructure Requirements

**5. What is your current GPU infrastructure?**
- Do you have existing GPU resources (on-prem or cloud)?
- What GPU types are you currently using?
- What is your current monthly GPU spend?

**6. What are your compute workload patterns?**
- Is your workload consistent or variable?
- What are your peak usage times?
- How predictable is your demand?

**7. What performance requirements do you have?**
- Required inference latency (ms)?
- Throughput requirements (predictions/second)?
- Availability requirements (uptime SLA)?

### Yearly Reservation Opportunity

**8. What is your expected usage over the next 12-24 months?**
- Growth projections for AI workloads?
- New use cases planned?
- Scaling timeline?

**9. Budget planning cycle?**
- Annual budget allocation process?
- Ability to commit to yearly reservations?
- CapEx vs OpEx preferences?

**10. Current pain points with compute?**
- Cost unpredictability?
- Availability issues?
- Management overhead?
